Noto is a global font collection for writing in all modern and ancient languages, with multiple weights and widths, in sans, serif, mono, and other styles. You can combine the Noto fonts with each other and with other fonts for harmonious, typographically correct communication in more than 1,000 languages and over 150 writing systems.

Noto Sans CJK JP is an unmodulated (“sans serif”) design for the Japanese language and other languages used in Japan. It covers Hiragana, Katakana, plus all Kanji characters from the Basic Multilingual Plane of the Unicode Standard, and some additional ones. It also supports Latin, Cyrillic, Greek and Hangul. It contains 65,535 glyphs, 27 OpenType features, and supports 44,806 characters from 55 Unicode blocks.
