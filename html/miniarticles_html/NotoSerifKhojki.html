Noto Serif Khojki is a modulated (“serif”) design for texts in the Indic Khojki script.

Noto Serif Khojki has multiple weights, contains 421 glyphs, 11 OpenType features, and supports 144 characters from 4 Unicode blocks: Khojki, Basic Latin, General Punctuation, Common Indic Number Forms.

Supported writing systems

Khojki

Khojki (𑈉𑈲𑈐𑈈𑈮) is an Indic abugida, written left-to-right. Used since the 16th century in today’s Pakistan and India by the Khoja people for religious texts in the Sindhi language. Needs software support for complex text layout (shaping). Read more on ScriptSource, Unicode, Wikipedia, Wiktionary, r12a.
